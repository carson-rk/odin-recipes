Sushi Rice

Description

Perfect sushi starts with perfect rice. This tried-and-true sushi rice recipe will guide you step-by-step to create the ideal base for all your sushi creations. With the right balance of vinegar, sugar, and salt, combined with the proper cooking and cooling techniques, you'll achieve that signature sticky texture and subtle flavor that defines authentic sushi rice.

Whether you're making classic rolls, nigiri, or creative sushi bowls, mastering this sushi rice recipe is essential. Follow along for tips on selecting the right type of rice, rinsing methods, and seasoning proportions to elevate your homemade sushi to restaurant-quality standards.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sushi or short-grain rice
  • 2 cups water (for cooking the rice)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Optional: A piece of kombu (dried kelp) for extra umami flavor

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice: Place the sushi rice in a fine-mesh sieve and rinse under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky.
  2. Soak the rice: Transfer the rinsed rice to a pot or rice cooker and add 2 cups of water. Let it soak for about 30 minutes to allow the grains to absorb water evenly.
  3. Cook the rice:Cook the rice according to your rice cooker instructions or bring the water and rice to a boil on the stove, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let it steam with the lid on for another 10 minutes.
  4. Prepare the sushi vinegar: While the rice cooks, combine rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small saucepan. Heat gently, stirring until the sugar and salt dissolve. Do not boil. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  5. Season the rice: Transfer the cooked rice to a large, shallow bowl (a wooden sushi oke or hangiri is ideal). Gradually pour the vinegar mixture over the rice while gently folding with a wooden spatula or rice paddle to evenly coat the grains without crushing them.
  6. Cool the rice: Use a fan or a piece of cardboard to cool the rice as you mix, which gives it a nice glossy finish. Once cooled to room temperature, the sushi rice is ready to use.
