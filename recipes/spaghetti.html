Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a timeless Italian pasta dish known for its creamy, indulgent sauce made from eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. It's simple to prepare but delivers rich, comforting flavors that make it a favorite in households around the world. Perfect for a quick weeknight meal or an impressive dinner for guests.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 50g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente, according to package instructions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  2. Cook the pancetta: While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic cloves and pancetta, cooking until pancetta is crisp and golden. Remove and discard garlic.
  3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Combine pasta and pancetta: Add the drained pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Toss well over low heat.
  5. Mix in egg mixture: Remove skillet from heat. Quickly add the egg and cheese mixture to the pasta, stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce without scrambling the eggs. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time if the sauce is too thick.
  6. Serve immediately: Plate the carbonara and finish with extra cheese and black pepper to taste.
